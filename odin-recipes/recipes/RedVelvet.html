Red Velvet Cookies

Description

Red velvet is a term used to describe a flavor that's a mix of cocoa and vanilla, often available in baked cookies, cakes, and cupcakes. The most distinguishing feature of red velvet is its vibrant red or deep maroon color.

Ingredients

2 1/2 cups of flour, 1 1/2 cups of sugar, 14 tbsp unsalted butter, room temp, 1 tsp white vinegar, 2 1/2 tsp vanilla, 2 tbsp unsweetened cocoa powder, 1/2 tsp salt, 1/2 tsp baking powder, 1 tbsp red food coloring , 2 eggs room temp, extra sugar to roll the cookies in before baking. Icing ingredients: 3 c powdered sugar, 8 oz cream cheese, 2 tsp vanilla, and 4 tbsp unsalted butter

    Step 1

    Using your electric mixer, combine the butter and sugar until it's smooth.

    Step 2

    Add the eggs, one at a time, to the mixer.

    Step 3

    Now add the vanilla, vinegar and food coloring.

    Step 4

    In a separate bowl, combine the flour, cocoa powder, baking powder and salt together.

    Step 5

    Slowly add the flour mixture to the wet mixture and mix until combined.

    Step 6

    Measure out a heaping tablespoon full of the dough. Roll the dough around in the extra sugar set aside to coat them, then roll the dough into balls and place on a baking sheet.

    Step 7

    Bake in an oven that's been preheated to 350Â° for 8-10 minutes.

    Step 8

    Remove from the oven and place the baking sheet on a wire rack to cool for a couple of minutes before placing the cookies directly on the wire rack to cool completely.

    Step 9

    To make the icing, using the electric mixer, combine the cream cheese and butter together.

    Step 10

    Now add the powdered sugar, a little at a time to the mixer.

    Step 11

    Now add the vanilla.

    Step 12

    Either cover the cookies completely with the icing, or pipe it on like I did.
